Veteran Artist Residencies

An Ink People Center for the Arts Dreammaker Project

The Veteran Artist Residencies non-profit provides post-9/11 combat veterans with paid artist residencies.

Military service has shaped and influenced generations of America's most influencial and powerful storytellers. From Pulitzer prizes to blockbusters, these artists draw inspiration from their experiences in the military and often have something meaningful to communicate--an urgent message to their fellow countrymen.

At the Veteran Artist Residencies non-profit, we believe all Americans benefit when veteran voices are heard.

Unfortunately, many returning veterans struggle to find stability, belonging, and economic self-sufficiency. And it seems sort of silly to dedicate your time to writing your masterpiece when you're struggling to survive.

This is a tradegy. Combat veterans possess the unique insight and literary potential that a faltering America needs now more than ever.

The mission of Veteran Artist Residencies is to tap into that potential by providing a space where promising post 9/11 combat veterans can live free from survival concerns and focus solely on their art.

Your donations go directly toward veteran artists monthly stipends and living expenses. A donation to Veteran Artist Residencies

Nate Hankes, the founder, discovered the healing power of art while writing his bestselling travel adventure and war memoir, Waking Up On the Appalachian Trail. He has dedicated a cottage on his property to provide his fellow veterans the opportunity to be heard.

The Mission

Between 2020 and 2023, the Veteran Artist Residencies non-profit intends to support four veterans by offering each a five-month fully-paid artist residency in beautiful and inspiring Humboldt County, California.

What type of artists are we looking for?

  • Novelists.
  • Memoirists.
  • Poets.
  • Screen play writers.
  • All forms of writing will be considered.

During the application process, we look for potential and ambition. A lot can be accomplished in five months, and we want our veteran artists to show up ready to immerse themselves in their artistic endeavors.
