Veteran Artist Residencies

An Ink People Center for the Arts Dreammaker Project

The Veteran Artist Residencies non-profit provides post-9/11 combat veterans with paid artist residencies.

"Vonnegut is a moralistic novelist but one who employs humor to drive home his ethical points. In many respects he most closely resembles Mark Twain not only in being a highly ethical novelist, but also in his use of comedy. His books serve a remarkable range of purposes: social commentary, theological discussion, ethical argument, parody, satire, and prophecy. His work reflects his strong belief in the dignity and worth of all individuals, and as an American pragmatist, he reminds his readers again and again of the unfinished nature of America." -Donald Morse

The truth is, the depth of emotional and psychological experiences experienced in war create some of the most perceptive, sensitive, and human story tellers our country has ever known.

But returning veterans often struggle to find stability, belonging, and economic self-sufficiency. And it seems sort of silly to dedicate your time to writing your masterpiece when you're struggling to survive.

This is a tradegy. Combat veterans possess the unique insight and literary potential that a faltering America needs now more than ever.

The mission of Veteran Artist is to tap into the squandered potential by providing a space where aspiring post 9/11 combat veterans can live free from survival concerns and focus solely on their art.

This organization provides paid artistic residencies to post-9/11 combat veterans. Your donations go directly toward veteran artist living stipends.

Nate Hankes, the founder, discovered the healing power of art while writing his bestselling travel adventure and war memoir, Waking Up On the Appalachian Trail. He has dedicated a cottage on his property to provide his fellow veterans the opportunity to reclaim their joy.

The Mission

Between 2020 and 2023, the Veteran Artist Residencies non-profit intends to support four veterans by offering each a five-month fully-paid artist residency in beautiful and inspiring Humboldt County, California.

What type of artists are we looking for?

  • Novelists.
  • Memoirists.
  • Poets.
  • Screen play writers.
  • All forms of writing will be considered.

During the application process, we look for potential and ambition. A lot can be accomplished in five months, and we want our veteran artists to show up ready to immerse themselves in their artistic endeavors.
